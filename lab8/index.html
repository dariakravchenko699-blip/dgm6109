Relationship between cycle phases and well-being characteristics

The original hypothesis is that cycle phases have different sleep, fatigue and stress characteristics. It would be assumed that menstrual (1) phase has lowest sleep count, largest fatigue and stress levels. Further, there could be a correlation between sleep and fatigue. We assume that "as sleep decreases, fatigue increases". And the same would be true for sleep and stress.

The presented scatterplot shows potential in relationships between fatigue and sleep, but is not very helpful in determining any relationship between cycle phases and well-being characteristics
